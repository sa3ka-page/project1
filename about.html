The Maldives is an island archipelago in the Indian Ocean. It lies to the southwest of India and Sri Lanka in the Akkadive Sea. A chain of twenty-six atolls extending from the Atoll to Addu Atoll. The capital and largest city is Male. Historically linked with the Indian subcontinent, the Maldives is a Muslim-majority country. Beginning in the mid-16th century, the islands were controlled by colonial powers. The islands gained their independence from the United Kingdom in 1965, and became a republic in 1968. The country is ruled by the president and his authoritarian government. The Maldives is economically dependent on tourism, and fishing, and includes a territory spread over about 90,000 square kilometers. The Maldives is one of the world's most geographically dispersed countries and the smallest Asian country in both land area and population. The Chagos Archipelago is located over the islands The Maldives-Akadiv Ridge, a submarine group of huge fortunes in the Indian Ocean, which also forms a terrestrial ecological, along with the Chagos and Lakshadweep. With an average ground level elevation of 1.5 meters above sea level, it is the lowest country in the world. The government has pledged to make the Maldives a carbon neutral country by 2019 amid concerns about rising sea levels. The islands are filled with palms and emerald green, surrounded by sparkling white beaches and warm waters. The surrounding coral reefs are home to an abundant variety of marine life, and the Maldives is also famous for snorkeling and diving.
